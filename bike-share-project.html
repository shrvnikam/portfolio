Bike-Share Analysis: How Do Annual Members and Casual Riders Use Cyclistic Bikes Differently?

Introduction

This case study focuses on analyzing Cyclistic's historical bike trip data to identify differences between casual riders and annual members. The goal is to provide actionable insights that can help Cyclistic convert more casual riders into annual members, as annual memberships have been identified as the most profitable revenue stream.

Given Situation

Cyclistic is a Chicago-based bike-share program with over 5,800 bicycles and 692 docking stations. The company offers flexible pricing plans:

  • Casual Riders: Use single-ride or full-day passes.
  • Annual Members: Subscribe to a membership plan.

Cyclistic‚Äôs finance team found that annual members are more profitable than casual riders. The marketing team, led by Lily Moreno, believes there is an opportunity to increase revenue by converting casual riders into annual members.

Approach & Steps Taken

To answer the key question, I conducted a Root Cause Analysis (RCA) and identified five specific research questions:

  1. Will converting casual riders to members increase revenue?
  2. How do ride durations differ between casual riders and members?
  3. During which time of the year are rides most frequent?
  4. Where do casual riders and members take rides?
  5. What is the total number of rides taken by each group?

I followed a structured data analysis process (Ask ‚Üí Prepare ‚Üí Process ‚Üí Analyze ‚Üí Share ‚Üí Act) to extract insights:

  • Data Collection: Utilized Cyclistic's publicly available historical trip data, ensuring privacy by excluding personally identifiable information. The dataset consisted of 20 million rows of trip data.
  • Data Cleaning & Processing: Worked with multiple large datasets to clean and format the data.
  • Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA): Identified patterns in ride behavior through visualizations.
  • Data Visualization: Used graphs and heatmaps to uncover trends in user behavior.
  • Insights & Root Cause Analysis: Found key differences in riding habits between casual riders and members.

Key Insights & Findings

  • Ride Duration: Casual riders take longer rides than members.
  • Seasonality: Casual ridership spikes in summer, while members ride consistently year-round.
  • Peak Usage Times: Casual riders prefer weekends, while members use bikes more on weekdays, likely for commuting.
  • Ride Locations: Casual riders frequent tourist-heavy areas, whereas members take rides more evenly distributed across the city.
  • Overall Ride Volume: Members take significantly more rides in total compared to casual users.
